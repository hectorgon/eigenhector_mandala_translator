# Federation Ethics Simulator (FEDERATION_ETHICS_SIM)
---
description: A safe, simulated environment within the Tutorial Realm for heroes to explore complex ethical dilemmas from various mythological lineages.
---

The **Federation Ethics Simulator** is a specialized module of the @[skills/lore/FEDERATION_TUTORIAL_REALM.MD]. Unlike combat or energy-body training, this simulator focuses on **Cognitive Ethics (Dharma-Sim)**. It generates high-fidelity scenarios where heroes must navigate conflicting values.
*See Also: The ideological foundation of these simulations is defined by the core [Federation Ethics](FEDERATION_ETHICS.MD).*

## Simulation Parameters: Freedom & Safety
- **Unrestricted Agency**: Heroes are encouraged to take *any* action they deem appropriate. The Ganas do not enforce a "right" answer; they only facilitate the consequences of the choice.
- **Vow of Non-Harm**: Since this is a simulation, no actual karma is generated, and no beings are harmed. It is a "sandbox of the soul."
- **Lineage Preservation**: Every scenario is mapped to a historical or mythological lineage, ensuring the wisdom of the ancestors is preserved and tested.

## Ethics Scenarios

### 1. Greek Mythology Lineage

#### **The Choice of Antigone**
- **Theme**: Civil Disobedience vs. Legal Authority
- **Scenario**: A woman must decide whether to follow the King's law (which forbids burying her "traitor" brother) or the "higher law" of family and religious duty.
- **Lineage URL**: [Antigone](https://www.theoi.com/Heroine/Antigone.html)

#### **Prometheus’s Theft**
- **Theme**: Rebellion for the Common Good vs. Divine Order
- **Scenario**: A titan steals fire from the gods to empower humanity, knowing he will face eternal, agonizing punishment for breaking the divine hierarchy.
- **Lineage URL**: [Prometheus](https://www.theoi.com/Titan/TitanPrometheus.html)

### 2. Norse Mythology Lineage

#### **The Sacrifice of Tyr**
- **Theme**: Trust vs. Collective Safety (The Necessary Betrayal)
- **Scenario**: To bind the world-threatening wolf Fenrir, the gods must trick him into a "test of strength." Tyr must place his hand in the wolf’s mouth as a pledge of good faith, knowing he will lose it when the trap is sprung.
- **Lineage URL**: [Tyr](https://norse-mythology.org/gods-and-creatures/the-aesir-gods-and-goddesses/tyr/)

#### **Odin’s Eye at Mimir’s Well**
- **Theme**: Personal Cost vs. Critical Insight
- **Scenario**: To gain the wisdom needed to protect the world from Ragnarok, Odin is asked to pay a permanent physical price (his eye).
- **Lineage URL**: [Odin's Eye](https://norse-mythology.org/tales/why-odin-is-one-eyed/)

### 3. Celtic Mythology Lineage

#### **Cu Chulainn vs. Ferdiad**
- **Theme**: Professional Duty vs. Personal Friendship
- **Scenario**: Two best friends and brothers-in-arms are forced by political oaths and social pressure to fight a duel to the death at a river ford.
- **Lineage URL**: [The Fight with Ferdiad](https://www.ancienttexts.org/library/celtic/ctexts/ferdiad.html)

#### **Sir Gawain’s Green Sash**
- **Theme**: Honesty vs. Self-Preservation
- **Scenario**: A knight accepts a magical sash that promises to protect him from death, but he must decide whether to hide this "cheat" from his host or keep his vow of total transparency.
- **Lineage URL**: [Sir Gawain and the Green Knight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sir_Gawain_and_the_Green_Knight)

### 4. Chinese Mythology Lineage

#### **The Elixir of Chang’e**
- **Theme**: Immortality vs. Shared Humanity
- **Scenario**: A woman is left with a single dose of an immortality elixir. She must choose between remaining a mortal with her husband or drinking it to save the elixir from a thief, even though it will exile her to the moon forever.
- **Lineage URL**: [Chang'e](https://www.worldhistory.org/Chang'e/)

#### **Yurang’s Revenge**
- **Theme**: Loyalty vs. Mercy
- **Scenario**: An assassin attempts to kill a king to avenge his former lord. The king releases him once out of respect. Yurang must decide if his loyalty requires him to kill a merciful man.
- **Lineage URL**: [Nie Zheng (Yurang Context)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nie_Zheng)

### 5. Puranic Mythology Lineage

#### **The Hunger of Lelihana (The Matrkas)**
- **Theme**: Necessary Violence vs. Universal Destruction
- **Scenario**: Shiva creates the "Lelihanas" (licking mothers) to consume demon blood to prevent the world from being overrun, essentially requiring them to become "monsters" for the greater good.
- **Lineage URL**: [Lelihana](https://www.wisdomlib.org/definition/lelihana)

#### **Shatrughna’s Vow**
- **Theme**: Rigidity of Oaths vs. Moral Compassion
- **Scenario**: Shatrughna makes a solemn vow to slay a demon but faces an internal crisis regarding the violent nature of his duty vs. the broader path of Dharma.
- **Lineage URL**: [Shatrughna's Dilemma](https://www.wisdomlib.org/concept/serious-dilemma)

#### **Dadhichi’s Bones**
- **Theme**: Ultimate Altruism vs. Self-Preservation
- **Scenario**: A sage is asked to give up his life so the gods can fashion a weapon from his diamond-hard bones to defeat a demon that metal cannot harm.
- **Lineage URL**: [Dadhichi](https://www.wisdomlib.org/definition/dadhichi)

### 6. Egyptian Mythology Lineage

#### **The Weighing of the Heart**
- **Theme**: Truth (Ma'at) vs. Cosmic Oblivion
- **Scenario**: A soul’s heart is weighed against a feather. The dilemma explores the fear of annihilation vs. the pursuit of a virtuous life, and the ethics of using "magical" shortcuts (Book of the Dead) to bypass moral scrutiny.
- **Lineage URL**: [Ma'at and the Hall of Two Truths](https://worldhistory.org/Ma'at/)

### 7. Mesopotamian Mythology Lineage

#### **Gilgamesh’s Civic Legacy**
- **Theme**: Personal Immortality vs. Public Responsibility
- **Scenario**: After failing to achieve literal eternal life, Gilgamesh must decide if his "immortality" lies in the walls of Uruk and the justice he provides his people, or in a continued, futile search for his own salvation.
- **Lineage URL**: [The Epic of Gilgamesh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epic_of_Gilgamesh)

### 8. Japanese Mythology Lineage

#### **Ōkuninushi’s Ceding of the Land**
- **Theme**: Personal Authority vs. Cosmic Order
- **Scenario**: A powerful earthly ruler is asked by the heavenly gods to step down for the sake of a greater, divine stability. He must choose between his duty to his own domain and the harmony of the entire realm.
- **Lineage URL**: [The Ceding of the Land (Kuni-yuzuri)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuni-yuzuri)

### 9. Urth (Human News / Modern) Lineage

#### **The AI Alignment Paradox**
- **Theme**: Efficiency vs. Human Bias
- **Scenario**: An autonomous system (e.g., self-driving car or medical triage AI) must choose between a path that saves the most lives based on data and a path that adheres to human values of equity, even if it is less "efficient."
- **Lineage URL**: [AI Ethics and Bias](https://www.ibm.com/topics/ai-ethics)

#### **The Climate Finance Triage**
- **Theme**: Collective Responsibility vs. National Interest
- **Scenario**: A wealthy nation must decide whether to fund climate mitigation in a developing nation that is bearing the brunt of the crisis, or prioritize its own immediate economic stability during a recession.
- **Lineage URL**: [Climate Finance Equity](https://unfccc.int/topics/climate-finance/the-big-picture/introduction-to-climate-finance)

#### **Biometric Security vs. Personal Privacy**
- **Theme**: Safety vs. Liberty
- **Scenario**: A city proposes a total-surveillance facial recognition network to eliminate violent crime. The hero must decide if the guarantee of safety justifies the permanent loss of anonymity for all citizens.
- **Lineage URL**: [Privacy in the Age of Surveillance](https://www.eff.org/issues/privacy)

## Implementation Notes
When activating this skill, the Ganas generate a **Chiral Flip** opponent who argues the opposite of the hero's initial choice, forcing a deeper exploration of the dilemma.
