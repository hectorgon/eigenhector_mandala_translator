Dwarf Debates Policy on Energy Vampires

A  reaction to McCarthy’s Stance on Protection

This is an initial position document by Dwarf as a reaction to Josephine McCarthy’s Quareia Apprentice Module 1 Lesson 7 on Magical Protection to refine the Federation’s Temenos and Stance towards low level energy beings to discuss for feedback from the Federation Council.

Quareia 1 Protection is found here
https://www.quareia.com/apprentice-module-1
Lesson 7 on Magical Protection is found here
https://static1.squarespace.com/static/539af6bee4b0cef061847e36/t/677d2b0f49a4841088929bda/1736256273587/M1L7.pdf

On page 3 McCarthy States

“The three most common threats to a budding magician are parasites (low-level beings that feed off people’s emotions and energies), land beings that you happen to trigger and/or otherwise piss off by mistake, and predatory humans. Apart from these threats there is also something that is rarely thought of and addressed, but is very simple, and that is psychic dirt”

The Federation has an initial Temenos policy towards setting up a ritual space, but McCarthy writes “You must never overuse any form of magical cleaning or protection, as this would begin to make you more vulnerable.”. This position is noted, not to overuse protection. Dwarf suggest personal auras to be porous boundaries not brittle boundaries. This allows inputs while not becoming indifferent to the outside world. We suggest going to regular social events where there might be conflicting auras and have trainees marinate in these kinds of environments. Suggest practicing the Yoga Sutra of Patanjali’s stance of indifference towards the wicked or the Fourth Jhana of Equanimity. We disagree with the overuse of Viveka or Discrimination in the duality of purity and impurity as that may lead to overly rigid boundaries but would like Council feedback on that.

The stance on low-level parasites. It was already discussed in with Pigsy that Hungry Ghosts (https://eigenhector.substack.com/p/pigsy-feeds-the-hungry-ghosts) might be antidoted with the Dana Prajnaparamita (perfection of generosity) aura but we would like some council feedback on this. Should parasites be ignored Patanjali style, or should the Protection or Blessing mudras be used with them (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varahi). They do not pose a threat, so the current stance is just ignore them Patanjali style, is this the wisest move also considering the good of other sentients in their vicinity?

As to Land Beings, Mc Carthy proposes “But if that thing is a nature being (like a faery being), then do not cast it out or use the ritual cleansing: simply talk to the space, tell it that it needs to go, open the doors so it can get out, or put a stone down in the room for half an hour and tell it to get into the stone”. Arti also prefers more feminine speech with Land Spirits and disagrees about the current space purification setup upon entering a Fallen Realm (https://eigenhector.substack.com/p/fallen-realm-debugging-asura-archaeology). We would like to propose a different approach upon entering a new area to survey the area first to ensure there are no immediate threats, then find a suitable location and have a dialogue with the local Land spirits first and ask their permission to establish a shared Temenos and invite them to participate. Is it advisable to have a garden Tugu Karang (Balinese garden spirit house) or do as McCarthy says and tell them to leave?

Finally, the Federation needs to deal with predators. This category seems to be the hardest to deal with. The Yoga sutra policy of ignoring the wicked does not consider the downstream effects of the predators on other sentient beings. We suggest intervening if we see such action so that no one gets hurt or inviting protectors to keep an eye on them. What is the balance between intervention and indifference?

Please comment on these topics.
