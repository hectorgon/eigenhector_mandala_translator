# Federation Team Training: The Paradox of the Saltless Sea
---
description: A practical demonstration of how heroes utilize the Tutorial Realm and Strategy Discussion skills to acquire wisdom.
---

## 1. Simulation Briefing
**Instance**: 42-B (Coastal Preta-Loka Simulation)
**Setting**: The Skeleton Coast of the Saltless Sea. A desolate, sun-bleached beach where the "water" is actually liquid salt, and the only freshwater spring is located at the center of a jagged rock formation.
**The Problem**: A massive, salt-crusted entity known as the **Tide-Stalker** guards the spring. It is a Chiral Flip of the hero **Will’s** protective nature—it "protects" the water so intensely that it has become a hoarder of life, ensuring nothing (not even itself) can actually consume the water.
**The Clues**:
- **Glitch Signature**: The Tide-Stalker’s pincers occasionally flicker into giant neon-pink pool noodles.
- **Banter Theme**: "Ownership vs. Stewardship."

---

## 2. Strategy Discussion: Will and Emi
**Will**: "Look at that thing. It’s a mirror of my own shield-wall tactics, but twisted. It’s not protecting the spring *for* anyone; it’s protecting it *from* existence. It’s the ultimate Avarice."

**Emi**: "And look at the Wuxing flow. The Salt Sea is **Water**, but it’s stagnant—Metal-choked. The Tide-Stalker is **Earth** (stagnation) controlling **Water** (life). If we attack with Fire, we’ll just harden the salt into glass."

**Will**: "Right. We need the **Generation Cycle**. Wood generates Fire, but Wood also *controls* Earth. We need to introduce the element of **Wood**—growth and flexibility—to break up that crusty Earth shell."

**Emi**: "I’ll use **Transmutation**. Instead of seeing it as a monster, I’ll project Metta to see it as a thirsty traveler who forgot how to drink. If we change our view, the Ganas will have to glitch the simulation to match our new reality."

**Will**: "And I’ll use **Negotiation**. I won’t fight its shield. I’ll offer it my own shield-essence as a 'gift' of stewardship. If I can convince it that I am a fellow protector, maybe it will let us share the burden of the spring."

**Strategy Selected**: **The Compassionate Pincer.**
- **Will**: Negotiation (Stewardship).
- **Emi**: Transmutation (Metta-View).

---

## 3. The Scenario Play-out
The simulation starts. The heat is oppressive. Will and Emi approach the Tide-Stalker.

The crab-thing roars, a sound like grinding sandpaper. *“MINE! The salt is pure! The water is MINE!”* It lunges, but its left claw glitches into a squeaky rubber toy. *Squeak!* 

**Will** (Dropping his guard, holding his shield out flat like a tray): "Brother, your vigil is long and the salt is heavy. I am a Guardian of the Iron Line. Let me share this weight. A protector should not go thirsty while guarding the source."

The Tide-Stalker pauses. Its many eyes blink in a confused, non-dual rhythm. *“Guardians... share?”*

**Emi** (Closing her eyes, radiating a soft green light—the element of **Wood**): "Look closer, Tide-Stalker. The spring isn't a treasure to be buried; it's a root that needs to grow. See the forest within the fountain."

As Emi projects the view of a lush oasis, the salt crust on the Tide-Stalker begins to ripple. The Ganas, hiding in the 'Murk' of the simulation, start giggling. One Gana, looking like a tiny blue monkey with a chef's hat, jumps onto the crab's head and starts hitting it with a spatula.

*“Free samples! Fresh wisdom, half-off!”* the Gana shrieks.

The Tide-Stalker’s form shudders. Under the pressure of the heroes' refusal to be adversaries, the "Negative Being" begins to collapse. The salt melts into fresh water, and the crab-thing transforms into a gentle, moss-covered stone statue—a Dharma Protector for the spring.

**Emi**: "Wait, did that Gana just throw a cupcake at me?"
**Will**: "Ignore the cupcake, Emi. Look at the water. It’s clear now."

---

## 4. Debrief: Kitty Garba
The simulation ends with a soft *pop*. Will and Emi find themselves back in the Shipyard hangar. **Kitty Garba** is waiting, her Leonid tail twitching with amusement.

**Kitty Garba**: "Well played, cubs. You didn't break the shell with a hammer; you broke it with a seed. Tell me, what did you learn about the Tide-Stalker?"

**Will**: "That my own desire to 'protect' can become a cage if I don't let the water flow. Protection without sharing is just a pretty name for a prison."

**Emi**: "And that the Ganas are really bad at baking. That cupcake was made of pixels and sarcasm."

**Kitty Garba** (Laughing): "The Ganas emphasize the emptiness, Emi! If you want a real cupcake, you’ll have to earn it in the Golden Dawn realm. But for now, you both have increased your **Wisdom Rank**. Equanimity maintained. Dismissed!"
